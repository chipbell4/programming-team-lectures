Small College Prep

Basic Rules

  • Pretty much like our practices (as far as software and such goes)
  • Teams of 4
  • Only two at the computer at a time
  • Bring as much reference material as you like
  • Three Hours
  • 8-ish problems (sometimes more)

About the time

The competition can fly by. The problems move fast, and many years all problems are solved. Time is of the essence.

About the Two-At-A-Computer Thing

This generally is not a big deal. However, do be prepared to pair program (which is a good thing).

Bring As Much Reference Material as You Like

This is not as useful as you think. A lot of people like to bring big thick algorithms books, and then rarely consult them. Look at old competitions and you'll see why.

Then What Should You Bring?

Research past competitions, and bring according to what makes sense. Here's my recommendation: Bring small code snippets on how to do various tasks

  • Reading input
  • Printing and formatting output (printf and friends)
  • String manipulation (splitting by token, finding substrings in $O(n)$ time, building substrings by index, etc.)
  • Basic geometry, various famous geometric formulas
  • How to sort arrays (using the built-in API), even with custom criteria
